Social Engineering Awareness

Social engineering is the art of manipulating people into revealing sensitive information or performing actions that compromise security. According to the survey, young adults are often vulnerable to fake job offers, impersonation scams, and emotional manipulation.

Common Tactics

  • Pretexting: Fake scenarios (e.g., HR asking for bank details).
  • Baiting: Free music, movies, or USB drives with malware.
  • Tailgating: Entering secure spaces by following staff.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be a boss, bank, or government official.

Protection Strategies

  • Verify identity of unknown callers or email senders.
  • Be skeptical of urgent or ‚Äútoo good to be true‚Äù offers.
  • Never plug in untrusted USBs or download unknown files.
  • Follow strict security protocols in offices and schools.
