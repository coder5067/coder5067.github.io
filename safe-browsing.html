Safe Browsing Awareness

Safe browsing means protecting yourself from harmful websites, downloads, and online scams. Many young adults (survey: 30% rarely verify websites) are at risk when shopping, banking, or browsing on public Wi-Fi.

Common Risks

  • Visiting fake or malicious websites.
  • Clicking pop-up ads that contain malware.
  • Downloading pirated software infected with viruses.
  • Using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.

Best Practices

  • Check for HTTPS (padlock icon) before entering information.
  • Verify domain spelling carefully.
  • Use ad-blockers to avoid malicious pop-ups.
  • Avoid downloading cracked/pirated software.
  • Always log out of accounts after use, especially on shared devices.

Modern Threats

  • Fake browser update popups tricking users to install malware.
  • Malicious Chrome/Firefox extensions stealing data.
  • Drive-by downloads from unsafe websites.
