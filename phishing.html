Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a Cyberattack in Which Attackers Pose as Trusted Organizations or Individuals to Deceive People Into Sharing Sensitive Information Such as Passwords, Bank Details, or Personal Data. It Remains One of the Most Widespread Online Threats, Especially for Young Adults.

Watch: What is Phishing? 🎣

Common Types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing: Fake emails pretending to be from banks or companies.
  • Spear Phishing: Personalized attacks using your name or workplace info.
  • Smishing: Phishing via SMS with malicious links.
  • Vishing: Fraudulent phone calls pretending to be support or banks.
  • Clone Phishing: Real emails copied but with fake links.
  • Pharming: Redirecting users from real websites to fake ones.
  • Crypto/Investment Scams: Fake offers with huge returns.

Real-Life Examples

  • Fake job offers asking you to click attachments → installs malware.
  • Discount shopping sites that steal money.
  • Public Wi-Fi traps capturing your logins.
  • COVID-19 scam emails about donations or vaccines.

Warning Signs of Phishing

  • Generic greetings like “Dear User”.
  • Urgent messages such as “Account blocked in 24 hrs”.
  • Suspicious domains (e.g., paypaI.com instead of paypal.com).
  • Unknown attachments or shortened links.
  • Requests for sensitive data by email/SMS.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify sender email before clicking links.
  • Hover over links to see the real URL.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  • Never use public Wi-Fi for banking.
  • Use a password manager for strong unique passwords.
  • Keep browser & apps updated.
  • Report phishing attempts immediately.

Emerging Phishing Trends

  • AI-powered phishing emails (perfect grammar, personalized).
  • Deepfake vishing – cloned voices of CEOs or managers.
  • Social media phishing via fake LinkedIn or Instagram profiles.
  • QR Code phishing (Quishing) redirecting to fake login pages.
